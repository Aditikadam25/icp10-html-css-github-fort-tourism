Towords The History....

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj built and seized many forts, which were a key part of his military strategy. These forts were designed to be difficult to approach, provide vantage points, and serve as safe refuges for civilians. Shivaji's forts are a symbol of Maratha power and reflect his military genius.
Some of the forts associated with Shivaji Maharaj include:

  • Raigad Fort: Shivaji renovated this fort and made it his capital in 1674. The fort is surrounded by valleys and is known as the "Gibraltar of the East". It features the Maha Darwaja, the King's durbar, and the Hirakani Buruj bastion.
  • Murud-Janjira Fort: Located on the Arabian Sea coast, this fort is considered one of India's strongest marine forts. It has cannons of both native and European make.
  • Torna Fort: Shivaji captured this fort when he was 16 years old. He used the treasure he found here to build the Rajgad fort
  • Sinhagad Fort: Shivaji captured this fort
  • Purandar Fort: Shivaji captured this fort

Pride Of Maharashtra➡️

Shivneri Fort

Shivneri Leni Walk, Junnar 410502 India
an ancient military fortification located near Junnar in Pune district in Maharashtra, India. It is the birthplace of Shivaji, the founder of Maratha Kingdom.

Raigad Fort

Raigad, Maharashtra 402305
Raigad is a hill fort situated at about 25 Km from Mahad in the Raigad district. Chhatrapati Shivaji renovated this fort and made it his capital in 1674 AD.

Sindhudurg Fort

Malvan, Maharashtra 416606
Sindhudurg Fort was the naval headquarters of the Marathas. Situated on Kurte Island, it lies half a km away from the Malvan Port in the Arabian Sea

Pratapgad Fort

Mahabaleshwar, Maharashtra , 412806,
Pratapgad is a mountain fort located in Satara district, in the Western Indian state of Maharashtra. The fort is situated 24 kilometres from the Mahabaleshwar hill station.

Harishchandrgad Fort

Harishchandragad Fort, Maharashtra 422604
Harishchandra Fort is a hill fort in the Ahilyanagar(Ahmednagar) district of India. Its history is linked with that of Malshej Ghat, Kothale village.

Purandar Fort

Gherapurandhar, Maharashtra 412205
Built during the Yadava dynasty in the 11th century, Purandar Fort was originally known as “Puranmadhye Gad” and served as a strategic outpost.
